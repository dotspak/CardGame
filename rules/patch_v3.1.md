# Pecto V3.1 Patch Notes

After testing out this current ruleset, it has been decided that most of the changes made in V3.0 will be used going forward. With a few changes after testing. Removing summoning sickness ended up being a huge success to push the game to a more aggressive playstyle; however, the game still had some moments where it was too stally and defensive, so these changes hope to fix that while also making the game more fun overall.

## Updates to Rulest

Offsites

- Offsites are now treated similarly to planeswalkers in MTG.
- When a player declares an attack that would damage a player, they can choose to instead attack the offsite card that player controls.
- When entering combat with an offsite, it is treated the same as entering combat with a unit, therefore offsite force now represents their damage.
- Offsites that say "Deals x bonus damage" deal damage in addition to their force.
- Note: Offsites still grant a LVL when destroyed.

Addon Keyword (offsites)

- The addon keyword no longer means it can only be played as an addon.
- You can now play addons in the main offsite zone, and then attach an addon to it.
- Addons attached to basic offsites still function the same.
- Stadium offsites still can't equip addons.

LVL Mechanics

- The LVL mechanic is being reworked as it was deemed to be too snowbally.
- While the mechanic was intended to be snowbally, it is almost impossible to come back after falling behind unless you draw the perfect cards.
- To help fix this, you now gain LVL when your life total is reduced via combat. 1 Life lost = 1 LVL gained, so bleed = 2 LVL gained.
- You cannot exceed the highest LVL value via this effect. For example, if you were at LVL 2, and your opponent was at LVL 3, if you take 2 damage you will not go to LVL 4 and will instead go to LVL 3.
- This is to ensure the mechanic is simply used as a comeback mechanic and can't let players sit and take damage to get ahead.
- You also no longer gain an additional LVL for going second.

Middle Row Buff

- Any unit placed in the middle row can now enter combat with any unit your opponent controls, but they can still only attack the opponent if the space in front of them is open.

Unit Movement

- Unit movement was made too weak and somewhat unintuitive with the change to only allowing adjacent lanes again, so it is being reverted.
- Units can once again move to any space and swap with any unit.

0 Force Rule

- Cards can now enter with 0 force. If a card enters with 0 force, they do not perish and instead act as if they were 1 force but deal no damage.
- If a card enters with more than 0 force and their force goes to 0 or less, they still perish. 
- This also applies if a 0 force card goes above 0 force.

Sick Status

- Cards you control that are sick now take damage at the start of your turn instead of the end.
- This is more inline with other card games that have similar mechanics and I think it is overall more clear.
- This should also hopefully buff the plague archetype a bit as they were largely negatively effected by the removal of summoning sickness.
- With this change you can now preemptively sicken cards and then immediately deal the damage when you pass your turn instead of the opposite.

Flying

- Flying is being changed due to the offsite change since it is now the attackers choice by default which was flying's previous effect.
- When a Flying unit declares an attack, they can first move to another lane before actually attacking.
- As usual with movement, static overwrites this. A static and flying unit cannot move when declaring an attack.
- Due to this change, offsites are losing flying again. *offsites technically have static anyways so this wouldn't do anything even if they had flying.*

New Keyword: Sealed

- A unit with sealed does not deal damage to players.
- This is usually given to balance overstated units.

## Card Changes

This time, i'm simply listing the cards that were changed and whether they were buffed, nerfed, or reworked. Please just ask if you want to see the changes.

General Changes:

- Due to the new changes to offsites making them less likely to perish, some cards have been changed to remove themselves after certain conditions, or have just been made easier to remove by choice in case you want to play a new card.
- Besides this, a lot of offsites have been updated to be more impactiful for staying on the field for longer periods of time. This should both make them better, as well as hopefully making player more likely to target them.
- The same changes apply to passive spells like Bountiful Harvest, where they could only be removed if you use all of their uses before which sometimes would never happen. Cards like this have been given conditions to delete them so you can play other spells. *I am debating adding infinite passive spells instead of this, but I don't like that idea.*
