# Basic Gameplay

## Required To Play

Each player requires:

- A deck consisting of 20 cards with no more than 1 card of the same name
- A 6-sided die to represent each player’s soul count (0-6)

## Terminology

- Deck: your collection of cards.
- Hits: The amount of times a player can take damage.
- The Grid: The 3x1 space on your side of the board where your creatures are played.
- Off-site space: The area where an off-site card may be placed.
- Titan space: The area where a Titan is played before being summoned.

### Start of the Game

When the game begins, each player shuffles their deck and draws 5 cards. Players may do one re-roll of this by shuffling their hand into their deck and drawing 5 new cards. After this, players decide the turn order by rolling the dice representing their soul count, whoever gets the higher number chooses whether to go first or second.

Each player starts with 4 souls and 5 hits.

### Each Turn

At the start of the turn, every player draws a card and gains 1 soul (players do not gain a soul on the first turn). If the player has more than 6 souls, they go down to 6. Players may place 1 creature on the grid per turn, and as many spells as they desire, so long as they pay the cost of the spell.  You cannot play a creature and override a creature in your grid. If you do not play a creature, you do not get an extra creature summon next turn; however, you use this creature summon during an opponent’s turn if an instant card requires it to be cast. When an off-site is chosen to be played, they are placed in a separate area from the grid. On a player’s first turn, creatures can only be played in the center space. Creatures cannot attack on the first turn they are summoned but can block and move.

There is no order to how the player must perform their actions, they may freely choose. For example, a player may choose to attack with a creature, then after this play a spell, then choose another creature to attack.

If at the start of the turn both the player's deck and graveyard are empty, they lose the game.

### Discarding Cards

When a card is discarded, unless stated otherwise, it goes to the graveyard pile. Creatures and off-sites are discarded when they perish, and spells are discarded when used. Cards can also be discarded through different special effects on cards.

When your draw pile is empty, shuffle your graveyard to reset your draw pile. Once a card is in the exile pile, unless stated otherwise it cannot exit that pile.

### End of Turn

A player’s turn ends when they pass the turn. At the end of a player’s turn, if they have more than 5 cards in their hand, they must discard cards from their hand until they have exactly 5 cards.

## Creatures/Off-Sites

### Basic Info

Creatures and off-sites all have names, types, stats, and keywords. A card’s name is in the box near the top of the card. A card’s types are listed next to the category of the card (for example, Creature – God, God would be the card’s type). The creature’s stats are in the box at the bottom of the card and is read as Strength/Life.

### Attacking/Guarding

When a creature attacks, they enter combat with the opposing creature. When 2 creatures engage in combat, both creatures take damage equal to the creature’s strength stat. If the opposing creature perishes, you gain 1 soul. Damage taken from combat does not automatically recover. If the opposing space is open, the opposing player can choose to defend with their off-site or take the hit. If they defend with the off-site, the two cards engage in combat the same way as 2 creatures. If the guarding creature survives, they are not considered to have “survived combat”, this only applies to attacking creatures who survive.

### Moving

A creature can move to an empty space adjacent to them, or swap places with another creature on the grid. When the creature moves to an empty space, they are tapped. When creatures swap places with each other, you may choose which of the two creatures are tapped A creature cannot move if tapped. Off-site cards cannot be moved.

### Abilities

Some creature/off-site cards have abilities that require souls to be used. Unless stated otherwise, these abilities can be used as many times as you can afford and can only be used on your turn. A creature can still use its ability while tapped, unless stated otherwise.

## Spells

### Normal Spells

If a spell has no keywords, they may be played only during your turn.

### Instant Spells

If a spell has the instant keyword, they may be played at any point in time. This can also apply to creature/off-site abilities.

### Effect Spells

An effect spell is equipped to the target and remains there until the card the effect is equipped to perishes, unless stated otherwise.

### Permanent Spells

Permanent spells remain on the board after they are played, and have no automatic discard unless stated otherwise.

## Keywords

### Positive Keywords

- Flying: Cannot attack or block a non-flying creature/off-site.
- Agile: Can Move to any open space on the grid.
- Energetic: Card can move and attack on the same turn.
- Bleed: Player hit takes an additional hit.
- Haste: Creature can immediately attack.
- Instant: Can be played at any point in time if the cost is met.
- Immortal: Cannot take damage.

### Negative Keywords

- Fragile: Card perishes after attacking
- Static: Creature cannot move
- Sealed: Cannot attack

## Counters

- Shield: Ignore damage taken, then remove a counter
- Sick: When a sick counter(x) is placed on a card, they are considered "infected". An infected card loses 1 sick counter at the start of the player's turn. When an infected card runs out of sick counters, they perish.

## Triggers

Triggers are yellow badges with pink text that represent an action that occurs when specific parts of the game occur

- Victor: Occurs when the entity survives combat.
- Ritual: The condition must be met in order to play the card.
- Perish: Occurs when an entity perishes.
- Begin: Occurs at the start of your turn.
- Once: Can be triggered once on your turn.
- Instant: The effect can be used at instant speed.
