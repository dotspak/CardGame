# LSD Rulebook

## Basic Setup

Each player requires a deck consisting of 20 cards, with no more than 1 copy of each card.
Each player starts at 5 hits, and 0 souls.

## The grid

Each player gets their own board known as "the grid". The grid consists of 4 main spaces:

- 3 Unit spaces (A,B,C) from left to right, where units can be placed.
- Off-Site space, where an off-site card can be placed.

## Units

Units are the most important card type in the game. Units are cards that are placed in unit spaces,
and are the main way to damage the player. Unless stated otherwise, all units cost 2 souls to play.
Every unit have 3 primary uses:

- Attack
- Defend
- Move

### Stats

#### Force

Every unit comes into play with a certain amount of force, defined by the number in the box at the
bottom of the card. Force is denoted by a number in a pink box if it is mentioned in a card's description.
Force is both a unit's health and damage. When two units engage in combat, they both lose force equal
to the amount of force the other unit has. If a unit's force is 0 or less after exiting combat,
they perish.

#### Bonus

The bonus stat is an extra modifier to a cards damage. When a card deals damage to another card, the
bonus stat is simply added on to their force stat to deal the final amount of damage.

### Actions

On your turn, a unit is able to move and attack once each. When a unit attacks, they attack the
space opposing them, and interact with whatever unit is there. If no unit is there, the opposing player
can either take damage, or block with an off-site card if they have one. Specifics for this are in the
off-site section.

## Off-Sites

Off-sites, compared to units are completely free to play barring any rituals; however, you may only
have one off-site occupying the off-site space at a time.

### Stats

Similar to units, off-sites also have force defined in the same area as units. Unlike units, force
only acts as an off-sites health, and they do not deal damage back to an attacking unit. The only
way an off-site can deal damage is if they have any bonus.

### Totems

Totems are a subset of off-sites that give you more options. If a totem is played in the off-site
space, it acts the same as any other off-site. The special aspect of totems is that one totem can
be played in addition to an off-site card. If a totem card is played this way, the two cards are
effectively combined, and their text is merged together. Both cards still use the stats of the
first off-site in play.

### Blocking

When a unit attacks an empty space, a player may choose to block with their off-site if they
have one in play. If you choose to do so, the two cards enter combat as defined in the stats section.
