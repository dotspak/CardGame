Overview

PectoTCG is a fast paced, high power card game where players play powerful cards to dominate their opponent through strategy and timing.

  • Players: 2
  • Deck Size: 20 cards, max 1 copy of each card name
  • Game Length: 10-20 minutes
  • Goal: Reduce your opponent's life to 0

The Grid, the main place where you play cards in Pecto, is divided into 5 zones. The front 3 zones is referred to as the frontline, and units placed there can deal damage to the opponent through combat. They also block any opposing cards. The back 2 zones is referred to as the backline. Cards in the backline cannot declare attacks, but are generally more protected than frontline cards. You typically play more supportive cards in the backline.

Card Types

Pecto consists of 3 main card types: Units, Icons, and Spells. All 3 card types are played from the hand or summoned through card effects. When a card exits the grid, they go to the discard unless specified otherwise.

Units

Units are the primary card type in Pecto and are you main way to win. Units are used to deal damage to the opponent. They can be placed in any empty zone in your half of the grid. When in the frontline, units can declare attacks. If there is a card in the opposing frontline zone, they will attack that card. If there is no card opposing them, you may choose to attack a card in the backline or the player directly. More specifics on combat are found in the dedicated section. Playing a Unit is referred to as summoning it.

Icons

Icons are the main supportive card type. Icons largely function the same as units, except they do not deal combat damage and cannot declare attacks. Typically icons are intended to exist in the backline giving passive benefits to your gameplan. Playing an Icon is referred to as summoning it.

Spells

Spells are a card type intended to give special effects and turn the tide of battle. They are played directly from the hand. You do not need any empty zones in order to play a spell card. Playing a spell card is referred to as casting it.

Tokens

Tokens are an extra card type that are created via card effects. When in play, they follow the same rules as units, except when they perish they enter the void instead of the discard.

LVL System

The term LVL referrs to 2 main things in Pecto. Both players have a stat called "LVL" which defines what kinds of cards you can play. Every card also has a LVL, defined by the light blue number at the top right corner of any given card. If your player LVL is less than or equal to a card's LVL, you may play it. Playing a card does not affect your LVL in any way.
In a similar vein is floating LVL. Floating LVL is a special resource that adds to your player LVL. Unlike your normal LVL, floating LVL is removed when the current turn ends.

Chaining

In addition to the LVL system, you can use the chaining mechanic to play cards earlier than intended. You may chain a card if it is 1 LVL higher or less than the highest LVL non-spell card you currently control. For example, if your LVL is 2, but you control a LVL 3 card, you can then chain a LVL 4 card into play. If a card's LVL is less than or equal to your current LVL, you cannot chain it into play.

Glossary

Keywords

Below is a list of keywords and all of their effects. Keywords are words highlighted with yellow italicized text.

Positive Keywords

  • Bleed: When this card deals damage, the opposing card perishes.
  • Flying: Cannot be blocked except by Flying or Warden cards
  • Quick: Deals damage first in combat.
  • Immortal: At the end of the turn, heal all damage taken this turn.
  • Protected: Cannot be targetted by destruction effects.

Negative Keywords

  • Static: Cannot be moved after being summoned.
  • Fragile: When this card takes damage, it perishes.
  • Delayed: Enters inactive.
  • Sealed: Cannot declare attacks.
  • Sick: At the start of your turn, take damage equal to your force/uses rounded up.
  • Doom: When this card perishes, it goes to the void instead of the discard.

Warping Keywords

  • Warden: Cannot deal player damage. When the opponent declares an attack, you may move this card to the opposing zone to block it if this card is active.
  • Instant: Can be played at any timing.
  • Effect: Must be played by attaching it to a card you control. Does not contribute LVL for chaining.

Triggers

Below is a list of triggers and when they happen. Triggers are represented by a golden badge with the trigger name.

  • Enter: When the card enters the grid.
  • Perish: When the card is destroyed by any means.
  • End: Triggers during the end step.
  • Begin: Triggers during the begin step.
  • Victor: Triggers when a card exits combat alive.

Special Counters

Below is a list of counters with unique effects. If not listed here, the counter does not have an effect and is simply used to keep track of something.

  • Stun Counters: The card cannot deal combat damage and is inactive while they have stun counters. Remove 1 at the end of your turns.
