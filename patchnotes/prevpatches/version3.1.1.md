# Pecto V3.1.1 Patch Notes

## Note

This is a small patch, think of this as an adendum to the previous patch. After thinking about some aspects of the game, I've rethought a few balance decisions in a couple key specific cases: these changes are listed below.

## Balance Update

Red Summon Mechanic

- The red summon mechanic is mostly fine, I think its a good mechanic for the game overall, but there was one notable oversight after removing summoning sickness.
- You can no longer sacrifice a unit for red summon if they have attacked that turn.
- Notably, this doesn't mean you can't sacrifice an inactive unit. You can still sacrifice a unit that used a tap skill.
- This is to fix the potental to OTK an opponent if they have an open lane simply using lvl 1 red summon units. You theoretically could've summoned a unit, attacked, saced it into a new red unit, attacked, saced that one into a new red unit, etc. This change is designed purely to counteract this.
- Note: this change only universally applies to red summon sacrifice specifically, not sacrifices in general, and this will hopefully not come up much.
- Another Note: This change is likely to be reverted in future after a more traditional life system is tested (if that life system ends up being good for the game), as a rule will be implemented there that would mitigate this being an issue regardless. This specific rule wouldn't be very fun with the current life system so I'm not implementing it for now.
- This also ended up creating another balancing decision listed next.

Sacrifice Balancing

- There are a few cards that were accidentally far too strong. With a notable card (that no one actually used for these means but was totally possible) being Gold Mine, that could endless sacrifice units at no real penalty.
- In many cases, these effects have been changed so the unit being sacrificed is required to be active.
- This mainly just applies to offsites and units with strong sac spells that you could also use to trigger OTKs due to the sac, not the effect. Gold Mine in particular being the notable outlier as mentioned.
- Specific cards effected by this change are listed below.

LVL Squash

- This may be a potentially controversial change, but I believe its healthy for the game overall.
- I've decided to largely reduce LVLs of units (and a few offsites) specifically. Alot of units, while being pretty decent if you got to a high enough LVL to play them, often just felt delayed by LVL as opposed to a reward for getting to the required LVL.
- Because of the removal of summoning sickness, there wasn't a huge reason to wait until LVL 6 and above to play your big guy when smaller units + a decent destruction spell is just as effective (especially when big guy dies to Sacrificial Knife).
- Any card previously LVL 3 and below was not effected by this, as well as most LVL 4 and 5 cards.
- Spells don't really have this issue as they usually have instant gratification just from being played, especially due to the lack of counter spells currently (lack of meaning 0).
- I also, more importantly, believe this change will just be more fun overall. Most of the really big (LVL 8+) units have decently consistent ways to cheat them out anyways so this will mostly affect decks that dont want to ramp constantly or play LVL cheating effects.
- Going forward I will be designing units to be within the LVL 1-5 range, only pushing to LVL 6+ for notably powerful effects or skills.
- Red Summon LVLs were not taken into consideration for this, but some were still changed. The higher LVL cards received more LVL decreases, while LVL 5 cards were either unchanged or went to 4, whereas LVL 6 cards may have gone to 4, etc.
- So, every unit (and some offsites) LVL 6 and above has been reconsidered and potentially reduced. This is not a universal change: specific cards are listed below.

Defender Keyword Update

- While the game was changed to make aggressive play the way to go, many decks still run defender cards that only exist to block, and defensive play in general has been quite weak lately. Due to this change, the defender keyword is being buffed.
- If an opposing unit were to attack an open lane, you may move a defender unit you control to block them. Due to the timing of this effect, it occurs after the flying unit movement trigger, so you can block flying units with defenders this way.
