# Pecto V4.0 Patch Notes - White Wind Update

## Overview

Pecto in its current state, has a lot of issues. Mostly card design related, but also overall design. For me, I think a particular pain point is the LVL system: its confusing, too snowbally, and limits card design. Some mechanics as well such as shield counters, offsites, and defender are either too weak, too strong, not fun, or a combination of these. This is a relatively nuclear patch that will completely change how the game is played going forward.

This patch aims to fix and rebalance:

- LVL System
- Offsites
- Life System

## Ruleset Updates

### Quick Updates

Defender Keyword

- Removed or replaced with sealed.

Bleed Keyword

- Now means that the opposing unit will always perish at the end of combat regardless of surviving or not.

Shield Counters

- Removed.

Red Summons

- Removed in favour of additional cost text.

Rituals

- Removed in favour of additional cost text.
- Most cards with rituals are losing their rituals, regardless of additional cost text addition.
- Most cards keeping rituals are now related to sacrificing units.

New Keyword: Delayed

- Introducing delayed as shorthand for "enters inactive" since a lot of cards have this effect.

New Keyword: Sick

- Explained in its respective section below.

Passive Spells

- Passives can now be targetted by generic card targetting effects. If they are targetted by destruction, they are simply removed, if targetted by damage, they lose uses equal to the damage.
- Alot of passives are getting buffed this patch, so this seems like an appropriate time to introduce this.
- It also allows players to remove their useless passives easier if needed.
- Passives still cannot be targetted by units (explained later)
- Note: while you technically could target them with most spells, most effects do not apply to passives.

### LVL System

- The current LVL system, as prevously described, has many issues. A card game's resource is maybe the most important thing to get right, and the old LVL system failed to be perfect.
- I am moving over to a new system inspired by the offsite card Tunnel of White Wind.
- With this system, your LVL is instead equal to the total LVL of cards you control. You can never go below LVL 1, but there is no real max LVL.
- Tokens count as LVL 0 cards unless specificed otherwise.
- The only exception to this is if a card is "banished" which is the case when a card has a red LVL icon.
- If a card is banished, it does not contribute to your LVL total.

Notable changes because of this system change:

- LVL is no longer a resource that can be spent, only gained.
- LVL is no longer gained via combat (this was very confusing).
- Offsites are much stronger since they have an inate resource gain (as well as further change).
- Cards now only range from LVL 1-5 (for now).
- There is no longer a "LVL gain" at the start of the turn, removing a trigger. The order now goes upkeep (start of turn effects), draw.
- The LVL 20 wincon is removed.
- The old mulligan system is being removed.

### New Mechanic: Floating LVL

- Some cards gain you "floating LVL".
- Floating LVL is a simple mechanic: you gain LVL equal to the amount of floating LVL you have until the end of the current turn, when floating LVL dissipates.

### Opening hand

- Due to the LVL system changing, you are now required to have at least 1 LVL 1 unit, offsite, or passive spell in your deck.
- You must now mulligan until you get at least 1 of these cards in your opening hand.

### Life System

- The old life system is being scrapped in favour of a more traditional life system.
- When you get hit by a unit, you take damage equal to their force stat.
- This opens up the design space for burn cards and healing effects. Some cards are being retooled to do this now.
- Due to this change, the Bleed keyword is now being changed to be equivalent to deathtouch in MTG.
- For now, the life total is being set to 10.
- This does cause games to be very quick and also the potential for OTKs, but I personally believe this to be more of a positive than a negative.
- This value was also chosen with the change to the middle lane below.

### Offsites/Backline

- Offsite keywords Stadium and Addon are being removed.
- The backline where offsites were previously played is being expanded.
- You can now play up to 3 offsites or passive spells in the backline (1 per slot).
- They all act independently of each other unless specified.
- Because of this, the middle lane now extends to backline for cards like the Onyx and Pearl golems.

### Middle Lane

- While you control a unit in the middle lane, you cannot take player damage from combat.
- You can still take direct player damage from burn effects.
- Offsites can still be damaged.
- Note: you can still attack the player if you want, but no damage will be dealt. This can be effective for victory triggers (Killer Bee, Clone Element, etc).

### Plague Archetype/Sick Counters

The plague archetype has been completely redone. Sick counters have been removed from the game, and are instead replaced with the keyword sick. Sick now makes the card lose half of its force at the start of the turn rounded up (meaning it'll kill 1 force units). Due to this, plague cards have been reworked or update accordingly. Some cards are leaving the archetype, like Plague Egg (now Evil Egg) and Conjoined to become much more generic.
