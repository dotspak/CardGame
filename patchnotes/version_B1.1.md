# Pecto Beta 1.1 Patch Notes

---

## Overview

After the Pecto pounding, some core issues with the game have come to light, most of which are card design issues. This update serves more as a shift in philosophy for balance as opposed to a full gameplay overall (although almost every card will be getting a tweak).

Here is a list of things that are being tweaked/removed this update:

- Card playability
- Destruction
- High access to recursion
- LVL reduction
- Free repeatable abilities
- Going second

---

## Balance Philosophies

### Destruction

Destruction has been proven to be an extremely difficult system to balance in Pecto and is almost always problematic. Due to this, destruction is being removed from the game in favour of direct damage instead.

Direct damage is a much healthier system in Pecto since stat buffing cards is the intended way for it to be played.

This also removes a piece of rules complexity/annoyance related to destruction.

Along with this change, if a card takes excess damage, the excess damage effectively does not exist. For example, Voodoo Demon says "When Voodoo Demon takes damage, add that many bleed counters to any target.". Voodoo Demon has 3 Force. If you dealt 5 damage to Voodoo Demon, it would only take 3 damage and add 3 bleed counters. 0 damage dealt is also no longer considered "dealing damage".

### Recursion

Recursion is a very strong mechanic in Pecto, but is also one of the more fun/interesting things about the game. Due to this, recursion is not being removed but it is being toned down substantially.

Things that recycle your deck are being untouched, as that is completely fine.

Cards that bring things back from your discard in anyway must have some cost beyond when you get the cards, whether that be discarding cards or losing life.

Many effects that add cards from your discard to your hand are becoming random instead of consistent (Add 2 random cards from your discard to your hand), as well as requiring the number of cards to be activated (so if a card says add 3 random cards from your discard to your hand and your discard has 2 cards you can't activate it).

### Life as a Resource

Life costs are being increased overall this patch, as 10 life is difficult to break through sometimes.

### Muted, Static, Delayed

Starting this patch I will be much more sparing with using these keywords, especially static (static is actually getting nerfed overall, more on this later).

### Tutoring

Tutoring is being toned down slightly, While tutoring isn't super problematic to the game, it is a pinch to strong currently. I will be hitting tutoring less overall since I believe mass recursion/self recursion is the larger issue at play.

### LVL Adjustments

This change I had been affectionally calling "Baby Pecto". From this point on, the max LVL a card can be is 3. LVL 4-5 cards have proven to be difficult to balance since they need to be good to justify them being played. Often what this meant was that they had to win the game immediately to be used, which to me signifies an unhealthy mechanic.

With cards ranging from LVL 1-3, it is much easier to balance cards around specific criteriom. For example, most LVL 1s are engines of some sort, LVL 2s are where you start getting solid damage/distruption and LVL 3s are your cards you build the deck around with big payoffs, but not quite as large as LVL 5s.

## System Updates

### Game Board

- Back row zones increased from 2 -> 3

This change makes it so now every front row card has a specific card to attack instead of having to choose while in the center row. It also allows players to play more cards overall, which has been a point of contention since moving to 5 zones.

### Attacking

When a card declares an attack, if the zone in front of them becomes empty before resolving the attack, you are then given the choice to attack the player or a back row card in the same column.

### Card Draw

The card draw system has been reworked to be less punishing and more engaging.

- At the start of your turn, draw 1 card. Draw an additional card if you control at least 2 back row cards.
- In addition to this, the first time you destroy an opponent's card during your turn (through any means), draw a card *(note: an opponent sacrificing or casting a card does not count as you destroying a card)*.

This means that in a given turn, you can draw up to 3 cards from the system alone (except the player going first, but they setup the tempo). This system (in particular the card destruction change) is to encourage players to actually engage with the opponent by breaking their board. In doing this, players are much more likely to have at least 1 open zone going into their turn meaning they can actually play a card that turn instead of just using their skills or attacking again.

### Going First vs Second

- The player going second can now attack on their first turn, but cannot attack the player *(note: I'm watching this very closely as the player going second has a clear card advantage with the new card draw system, but this is likely fine)*.

### 0 FORCE cards

The old "no FORCE" concept is returning to 0 FORCE cards. Cards with 0 base FORCE now have a greyed out FORCE icon and no number. This means they cannot gain FORCE through any means, and remain as 0 FORCE permanently. With this change, every card intended to fight now has at least 1 FORCE.

## Keywords

### NEW Keyword: Spell

The new Spell keyword is replacing the "response" keyword. A Spell card can be set as a response. If you do so, the opponent is first given a response. After they conduct or pass their response, you may then activate the spell card.

### NEW Keyword: Leech

If a card with Leech hits the opponent, HEAL 1 *(note: this means the card doesn't need to deal damage to get the heal)*.

### Bleed

- Bleed Counters are now removed when bleed damage is dealt.

### Effect

- Removed.

Effect has proven to be largely useless as well as quite confusing so for now it is being removed entirely. Due to this, some cards are going to be replaced outright since they only function as effect cards. Attaching cards (Soul Lantern, Crow Devourer) still exists, but its effectively just an "extra discard pile" attached to a card. I may reevalute this later.

### Muted

- Cards with muted are now unable to target the player for attacks. They may still target back row for attacks.

### Static

- Cards with static now enter inactive and never become active. This keyword is now used on cards with the strongest passive values that you typically just place in the back row and never want to touch again. It is very sparingly used as it can be a very boring keyword to play with, but its a great balancing lever.
